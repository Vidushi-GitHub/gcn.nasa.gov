**Launch Date:** January 11, 2026

**End of Operations:** Mission duration of 1 year (with possible extension)

The Black hole Coded Aperture Telescope (BlackCAT) is a NASA 6U CubeSat mission developed by Penn State (Principal Investigator, Prof. Abe Falcone). BlackCAT is designed to monitor the soft X-ray sky (0.5 - 20 keV) with a wide field of view telescope, enabling the rapid detection and localization of high-energy transients.

The primary science objective of BlackCAT is the discovery of high-redshift gamma-ray bursts (GRBs), gravitational-wave counterparts, and other transient events. BlackCAT will provide arcminute-scale localization for Gamma Ray Bursts, gravitational-wave counterparts, high energy transient events and monitor galactic and extragalactic variable sources. BlackCAT also functions as a wide field X-ray transient monitor, providing rapid alerts for multiwavelength and multimessenger follow-up.

**JSON-Serialized GCN Notices Types in GCN Kafka**

BlackCAT will distribute alerts for gamma-ray bursts and other high energy X-ray transients via GCN Kafka topic `gcn/notices/blackcat/xray_transient_alert.`

BlackCAT [GCN schema](/schema/stable/gcn/notices/blackcat/xray_transient_alert.schema.json) and
example [JSON message](/schema/stable/gcn/notices/blackcat/xray_transient_alert.example.json) files are available. See the [Schema Browser](/docs/schema/stable/gcn/notices/blackcat/xray_transient_alert.schema.json) for more information on the properties defined in the schema. Detailed description and examples of BlackCAT Notices are available in the [GCN Schema GitHub project](https://github.com/nasa-gcn/gcn-schema/tree/main/gcn/notices/blackcat/xray_transient_alert).
